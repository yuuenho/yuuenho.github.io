About Me
I’m a Toronto-based UX designer.

I originally started my career working with students with disabilities. My role was to engage with students and to examine the barriers they experienced attending university. I worked with these students to develop solutions and coordinate services to make their education more accessible. Seeing what the students I worked with were able to accomplish when those barriers were removed showed me what can be done when inclusive design empowers users. Though it's not always possible to design for everyone, my belief is that when things are designed with fringe audience in mind, it often includes a greater population as result.

In order to start being more inclusive, just like in any other user-centric design approach, is to just start asking users about their experiences and draw from that to understand the problem space.

In addition to my UX skills, one of the things I've been working and developing is my coding abilities. I'm very proud to say that this website is my first coded project.

Over the years, I started making costumes. I love where that's taken me, from sewing costumes traditionally now moving into prop making. It’s allowed me to explore different methods and approach all my costumes quite uniquely. I recently made a pulp sci-fi space helmet outfitted with lights and a personal fan to keep things illuminated and cool. My next project will be working with motors and creating an animatronic monster.

Depending on the project I prototype in Sketch, Flinto, inVision, and HTML5 + CSS3
